Overview

NutrientKG is a food-health knowledge graph providing general infrastructure for disease-aware recipe adaptation across chronic disease conditions. Many chronic diseases impose formally defined dietary constraints that evolve as disease progresses through clinical stages. NutrientKG provides an extensible backbone integrating culinary knowledge, nutritional data, validated substitution pairs, and disease staging constraints into a unified queryable resource. In this initial version, NutrientKG is instantiated for Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD), with the CKD implementation serving as a reference for extension to other conditions such as diabetes, heart failure, and hypertension.

General vision: NutrientKG is designed as an extensible backbone for any chronic disease with formally defined dietary guidelines. The CKD instantiation is the first reference implementation. Future instantiations for diabetes, heart failure, and hypertension are planned, and community contributions of additional disease instantiations are welcome.

Key capability (CKD instantiation): NutrientKG can identify ingredient substitutes safe not only at a patient's current CKD stage but across all future stages of disease progression, a query class no existing food knowledge graph supports.

NutrientKG integrates seven data sources into a unified queryable graph:

USDA Nutritional Data

Comprehensive nutrient profiles including phosphorus, potassium, sodium, and protein critical for CKD dietary management.

Ingredient1M

Large-scale recipe dataset providing culinary context and ingredient co-occurrence relationships.

Recipe1MSubs

Validated ingredient substitution pairs providing ground truth for substitution recommendation.

NKF Kidney-Friendly Recipes

227 clinically validated recipes from the National Kidney Foundation, enriched with CKD stage-specific annotations derived from KDIGO guidelines.

FoodOn

Food ontology used as a pivot for cross-dataset entity alignment and semantic food classification.

SNOMED CT

Clinical terminology backbone grounding disease-food relationships in a standardized medical coding system.

KDIGO CKD Staging Constraints

Formally encoded dietary staging constraints from KDIGO clinical guidelines specifying nutrient limits per CKD stage (3b, 4, 5).

NutrientKG defines the following primary entity types and relations:

Entity Types

Query Examples

NutrientKG supports four classes of food-health queries via SPARQL. The following examples illustrate queries that existing food knowledge graphs cannot answer.

Q1: Retrieve validated substitutes for an ingredient

SELECT ?substitute ?context WHERE {
  ?ingredient rdfs:label "chicken"@en .
  ?ingredient nutrientkg:canSubstitute ?pair .
  ?pair nutrientkg:substituteIngredient ?substitute ;
        nutrientkg:recipeContext ?context .
}

Q2: Find NKF-validated recipes safe for a specific CKD stage

SELECT ?recipe ?title WHERE {
  ?recipe a nutrientkg:NKFRecipe ;
          rdfs:label ?title ;
          nutrientkg:safeForStage ?stage .
  ?stage rdfs:label "CKD Stage 4" .
}

Q3: Find substitutes safe across the full CKD progression trajectory

SELECT ?substitute WHERE {
  ?ingredient rdfs:label "chicken"@en .
  ?ingredient nutrientkg:canSubstitute ?pair .
  ?pair nutrientkg:substituteIngredient ?substitute .
  MINUS {
    ?stage a nutrientkg:CKDStage ;
           nutrientkg:stageOrder ?order .
    FILTER(?order >= 3)
    ?stage nutrientkg:hasConstraint ?constraint .
    ?constraint nutrientkg:nutrientType ?nutrient ;
                nutrientkg:maxValue ?limit .
    ?substitute nutrientkg:hasNutrient ?fact .
    ?fact nutrientkg:nutrientType ?nutrient ;
          nutrientkg:value ?value .
    FILTER(?value > ?limit)
  }
}

Q4: Detect substitutes safe today but harmful under disease progression

SELECT ?substitute ?futureStage WHERE {
  ?ingredient rdfs:label "chicken"@en .
  ?ingredient nutrientkg:canSubstitute ?pair .
  ?pair nutrientkg:substituteIngredient ?substitute .
  ?currentStage rdfs:label "CKD Stage 3b" ;
                nutrientkg:hasConstraint ?cc .
  ?cc nutrientkg:nutrientType ?nutrient ;
      nutrientkg:maxValue ?currentLimit .
  ?substitute nutrientkg:hasNutrient ?cf .
  ?cf nutrientkg:nutrientType ?nutrient ;
      nutrientkg:value ?value .
  FILTER(?value <= ?currentLimit)
  ?futureStage a nutrientkg:CKDStage ;
               nutrientkg:stageOrder ?order .
  FILTER(?order > 3)
  ?futureStage nutrientkg:hasConstraint ?fc .
  ?fc nutrientkg:nutrientType ?nutrient ;
      nutrientkg:maxValue ?futureLimit .
  FILTER(?value > ?futureLimit)
}
